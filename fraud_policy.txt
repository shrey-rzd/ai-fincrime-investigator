[HIGH VALUE TRANSACTION RULES]
Transactions above INR 200,000 are considered high-risk and require enhanced due diligence.
Transactions between INR 100,000 and INR 200,000 require monitoring and pattern analysis.

[TRANSACTION TYPE RISK]
CASH_OUT transactions are commonly associated with fraud and rapid fund extraction.
TRANSFER transactions are considered medium to high risk, especially when combined with high value.
PAYMENT transactions are generally low risk but can become risky when frequency or value increases.

[VELOCITY PATTERNS]
Multiple transactions in a short timeframe indicate potential fraud or laundering.
Rapid movement of funds across accounts is considered suspicious behavior.

[BEHAVIORAL INDICATORS]
Unusual transaction patterns compared to historical behavior indicate potential fraud.
Sudden increase in transaction size or frequency must be flagged.

[AML (ANTI-MONEY LAUNDERING)]
Layering of transactions (multiple intermediate transfers) indicates potential laundering.
Transactions without clear economic purpose are considered suspicious.
Repeated transfers across accounts may indicate structuring.

[ESCALATION GUIDELINES]
Transactions with combined indicators (high amount + risky type) must be escalated.
Borderline cases must be reviewed manually.
All high-risk flagged cases should be investigated further before decision.
